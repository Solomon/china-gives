Guangdong – Land of Charity

The southern province is home to 21 philanthropists, making it the most generous region. He Xiangjian, founder of electrical appliance manufacturer Midea, leads with 410 million donation in the 12 months period. Top 100 philanthropists are located in 21 municipalities/provinces. Guangdong is followed by Beijing (16), Zhejiang (11) and Fujian (8).

Real Estate Gives

China’s booming property market has minted the most philanthropists. There are 32 individuals whose core business is building houses, nearly twice as the next most generous sector: manufacturing (18). Consumer (15) and technology (14) sector rank the third and fourth, respectively.

Education Takes

Education gets the lion’s share with 81 individuals giving to schools or students. Eight Chinese universities received donations of at least 100 million yuan. Poverty alleviation and social welfare is the second most active sector (62), followed by disaster relief (35).

Government

Two out of five philanthropists (41) donate to government or government-backed charities, highlighting the trust donors put in government, despite a series of scandal that undermined the credibility of the country’s governmental charity several years ago.

About the Research

The rise of private wealth is one of the most important developments in modern China, with implications for the country’s social, economic, and political arenas. How individuals choose to deploy such resources will shape the relationships between the individual and the state, between the state and business, and between the state and the social sector.

The research aims to provide a holistic view of China’s philanthropic landscape by analyzing specific givings of China’s most generous individuals. The findings are based on a variety of sources, including media reports, company websites, corporate filings and trade journals and most of them they are verified by each philanthropist or his or her representative and recipients of the funds. All information is separately verified by China Charity & Donation Information Center, an affiliation of China’s Ministry of Civil Affairs.

We believe that an independent, verified, and research-oriented design of a Chinese giving database will enable much-needed quantitative research and will also result in indices that can be used by a range of government, academic, media, and non-profit organizations.

This website will be updated every year. The 2015 report is compiled by: Peiran Wei (MC MPA '15, Harvard Kennedy School), Zijia Guo (MIB '15, The Fletcher School of Tufts University), Qinqin Wu (MDes '15, Harvard Graduate School of Design), Cuimei Zhang (Ed.M 15', Harvard Graduate School of Education) and Evan Kornbluh (MPP/MBA Candidate, Harvard Kennedy School/Harvard Business School).
